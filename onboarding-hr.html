Onboarding Kit

How joining works today

Prepared for your company · for whoever runs HR day to day

This form is about how new joiners actually start with you today, not how it should look on paper. Honest answers make the kit useful: where something does not exist yet, saying so is exactly what we need, because the gaps are what we help you fix.

This is not here to find fault with you or anyone else. It is here to help HR and the company get onboarding streamlined and steer clear of trouble later. It takes about ten minutes, and you can send it in one sitting. Where a question does not apply, say so and move on.

When it is nobody's job, every joiner's start depends on luck; naming the owner is step one of the kit.

02

Paperwork and the law

H3When someone joins, is their personal file completed and their appointment letter issued promptly, on the first day or within the first week?

A complete file and a prompt letter protect the company in any later dispute; the kit makes both automatic and tells the joiner at the offer stage what to bring.

H4Are EPF enrolment and the nomination form completed on the joiner's first day?

These are due from day one by law; doing them at the start costs minutes and prevents penalties and stalled claims later.

H5Does every new joiner get their probation terms in writing, and a confirmation letter when probation ends?

Written terms and a prompt letter mean probation protects you instead of quietly lapsing.

03

Settling in

H6During probation, does the manager review the joiner at set points, or only when the end date arrives?

Mid-probation check-ins prevent end-of-probation surprises, for you and for the joiner.

H7Who teaches a new person the actual job, and how does that usually happen?

The kit assigns this deliberately, so learning the job stops depending on who happens to be free.

H8What do new joiners most often ask about, or complain about, in their first month?

Their first-month questions become the first-week plan and the handbook page, so the next joiner does not have to ask.
