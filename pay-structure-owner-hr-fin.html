Prepared for your company · for the owner, HR and whoever holds the money numbers

This short form starts your pay structure engagement. It asks how pay works in your company today and what you want it to do; it does not ask for anyone's salary. Salary information comes separately, through the confidential channel described in our document request, and is handled under the confidentiality terms in your engagement letter. The structure we build starts from what you already pay, so it will look like your company rather than a textbook, and becomes your own reference for recruiting and for future pay decisions. The form takes about fifteen minutes. If one person wears more than one of the three hats below, answer all the parts that apply.

On confidentiality. No field on this form asks for a salary figure. The individual pay data is sent only through the private upload folder linked at the end, created for your engagement alone. It is stored only in your engagement file, is never quoted with names in any report, and is never reused, benchmarked or shared without your written consent. 01

For the owner or managing director

Your view of what pay here should reward.

M1When pay goes up in your company, what should it reward? Think of the last raise you were glad you gave, and one you regretted.

Your answer becomes the rules of the structure, so pay rewards what you actually value rather than a textbook model.

M2What pay arguments keep coming back? For example, two people doing the same job on different pay, or raises going to whoever asks hardest.

The structure is built to end your specific recurring arguments, not generic ones.

M3Have you lost people over pay in the last two years? To whom, and for roughly what kind of difference?

This tells us where the market genuinely bites, so the ranges defend the roles you actually risk losing.

M4Are there people whose pay you already suspect is out of line, high or low? You may name them here or keep this for our sit-down. (Optional.)

Knowing where you expect problems lets us check those cases first and cost the fix honestly.

M5Should bonuses or commissions be part of this design, or is fixed pay the priority for now?

An incentive scheme only works on top of a sound base; telling us your priority keeps the engagement focused and its worth visible.

M6When this engagement is done, what should be different?

This becomes the test we hold the whole deliverable against.

02

For whoever runs HR day to day

Logistics only. No pay figures here; those go to the upload folder at the end.

H1Who holds the pay records, and in what form? Payroll software, Excel, or paper.

We shape the data request around what you already have, so nobody retypes anything.

H2For the salary data you send us, would you prefer real names or codes? With codes, you keep the key and we never see names against figures.

Either way works for the analysis; choosing now means the data arrives once, in the form you are comfortable with.

H3List your roles and how many people are in each, or attach any staff list by role.

The role list is the skeleton of the whole structure; starting from your real roles keeps every later step grounded.

If you have a staff list by role, add it to the upload folder at the end. A photo or export is fine.

H4Is there any written salary scale, grade list or banding today, even a rough one?

If something exists we build on it, so the new structure feels like an upgrade rather than a replacement.

If yes, add it to the upload folder at the end.

H5When was the last company-wide increment round, and how were the amounts decided?

The transition plan starts from where the last round left everyone, so nobody's recent raise is accidentally undone.

H6What bonuses, commissions or regular allowances are paid today, and are the rules for any of them written down? No amounts needed here.

What is already promised shapes what can change; knowing the rules, written or unwritten, prevents the new plan breaking an old promise.

If any scheme is written down, add it to the upload folder at the end. No amounts needed on this form.

03

For whoever holds the money numbers

The owner or whoever handles finance. Round figures are fine; no per-person pay here.

F1Roughly what does pay cost per month in total, including EPF and ETF? A round figure is fine.

This sizes every costing in the report against your real pay bill, so recommendations arrive affordable rather than theoretical.

F2Is there a ceiling on what next year's increment round can cost? A figure or a percentage, if one exists.

The transition plan is phased to fit your ceiling, so the structure is one you can actually implement.

F3Today, are EPF and ETF paid on basic salary only, or on basic plus allowances?

This affects the true cost of every option we design, and it lets us flag anything worth checking before it becomes expensive.
