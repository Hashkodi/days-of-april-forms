02

Hiring and first days

3.7Last time you interviewed, did the interviewers agree the questions beforehand?

Agreed questions make comparing candidates fair and fast.

8.1Think of your newest team member's first day: was everything ready for them (desk, tools, accesses, paperwork)?

First days set the tone for everything after.

8.2Did a named person welcome and orient them?

8.4Did they know what to do in week one without guessing?

8.5Was someone assigned to answer their everyday questions?

8.6Did you review them during probation, or only when the confirmation letter was due?

Mid-probation check-ins prevent end-of-probation surprises.

8.7Did anyone ask them how joining felt, and act on what they said?

03

Performance and growth

10.5After a good review, does anything visible happen (pay, promotion, development)?

Reviews that lead nowhere teach people to stop trying.

10.8Could anyone on your team be surprised by a bad rating?

The honest answer should be no; surprises mean feedback is not landing.

11.2What training did your team get this year, and what problem did it solve?

11.3How does a new joiner actually learn the job here?

11.6Do your senior people deliberately pass on what they know (sessions, shadowing, notes)?

04

Fairness and one-to-ones

13.4Before anyone on your team was disciplined, were they given a chance to explain their side?

Being heard first is both the law's expectation and basic fairness.

14.4Do you talk to each of your people individually on some regular rhythm?

Ten minutes one-to-one prevents most resignations that "came out of nowhere".
