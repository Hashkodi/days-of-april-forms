This short form is where your KPI kit starts. Your answers decide which numbers this business should run on, so nothing we build is generic. It takes about ten minutes. There are no wrong answers, and "we do not track that" is a perfectly useful one.

This is not a test of the business. It simply maps what you already have, so the kit we build fits you rather than a textbook. Nothing is saved on this device until you send it at the end.

We build on the numbers you already trust instead of replacing them. For example daily sales, the bank balance, or the monthly accounts.

M5Is there a number you tend to find out about only when it is already a problem?

Those are exactly the numbers the kit is designed to show you earlier.

M6Do any roles already have written targets or KPIs?

If something exists we keep what works, so the kit feels familiar to your people rather than imposed. If you have an example, add it to the upload folder at the end; a photo is fine.

03

The decisions you want numbers for

M7What decisions do you currently make on gut feel that you would rather make on numbers?

Every KPI we propose must feed a decision of yours; this answer is the test we cut them against. For example who deserves a raise, when to hire, or which product to push.

M8When someone asks you for a raise or a promotion, what do you look at to decide?

Role targets turn this conversation into facts, which is fairer on you and on them.

M8aDo you give staff a yearly salary increment?

Knowing how pay moves here tells us where the numbers we build will matter most to your people.

M8bIs that increment linked to performance or a review, or is it the same for everyone?

If pay rises are meant to reward performance, the targets we set become the fair, defensible basis for that decision, instead of an argument each year.

M9If this kit did one thing well, what should it be?

We weight the whole engagement toward your answer.
