01

What numbers exist and where they live

H1Where are sales figures recorded, and how soon after the month ends could you produce them?

The kit only uses numbers that can be pulled without extra work; this tells us what is realistic.

H2Is daily attendance recorded for everyone?

Attendance is usually the easiest early-warning number a company already owns.

H3Could you say how many people left in the last 12 months, without a long search?

Staff turnover is one of the few numbers that predicts trouble; if it is hard to pull, we will show you a one-line record that fixes that.

H4Does anyone track money owed by customers and how old those debts are?

Debtor days is often the number an owner most wishes they had watched sooner.

H5Is there a monthly report or numbers pack that goes to the MD now?

We design the scoreboard to slot into what already reaches the MD, not to add a second pile of paper. If you have a recent one, add it to the upload folder at the end.

H6Which numbers does someone ask you for again and again?

Repeat requests are a sign a number belongs on the standing scoreboard, so you stop compiling it by hand each time.

02

How roles and reviews run today

H7Do roles here have written job descriptions?

The role sheets we build double as simple written roles where none exist, two problems solved at once; where they exist, we build straight from yours. If you have one or two examples, add them to the upload folder at the end; a photo is fine.

H8When performance is reviewed, is it against anything written, or from memory and impression?

Written targets are the company's protection: a rating or a raise decision can be defended on an agreed number, and the company is covered if a decision is ever challenged.

H9Is there a regular management meeting where numbers are discussed?

If a meeting exists, the review rhythm we design uses it instead of adding a new one to your calendar.
