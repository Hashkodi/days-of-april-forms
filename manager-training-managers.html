How things work for you now

You are taking part in a short practical training series about managing people. This form is not a test and has no right answers. It simply tells us how things work for you now, so the sessions fit your real situation instead of a textbook one. Your answers come to Days of April to plan the sessions; they are not given to your management as a report on you.

Nothing here is scored, and nothing you write is shown to the MD as an assessment of you. Answer as things really are. It takes about seven minutes.

01

How you came to manage

G1How did you become a manager here? For example promoted from the team, hired into the role, or the role grew around you.

Most managers here were promoted for being good at the work itself; knowing your path helps us start from where you are.

G2How many people report to you, and roughly how long have you managed them?

Team size decides which tools fit you; managing three people and managing fifteen are different jobs.

02

How you run your team today

G3Do you talk with each of your people one-to-one on some regular rhythm?

If you already do this, the sessions sharpen it; if not, we build it with you from zero, which is where most attendees start.

G4When you have to tell someone their work is not right, how do you usually go about it?

There is no right answer here; your real approach is the starting point the feedback session works from.

G5Do the people in your team have clear targets that you could both point to?

If targets are fuzzy, that is the single most common finding, and one session is dedicated to fixing it.

03

Where you want more practice

G6Which of these situations do you find hardest? Tick any that apply.

The sessions give extra weight to whatever the group ticks most; your honest ticks buy you more practice where you want it.

G7What would make this training worth your time? What do you want to be better at afterwards?

We read every answer before session one and shape the examples around them.
