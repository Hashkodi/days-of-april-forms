Discipline & Termination Toolkit

How it really runs

Prepared for your company · for whoever runs HR day to day

Please be assured this is not about finding fault with anyone. Its only purpose is to help you and the company put clear, fair steps in place so that discipline and exits run smoothly and no one is caught out later. The gaps you tell us about are simply what we build for you. Everything is treated confidentially.

This form is about how discipline and exits actually run, not how they should run. Where something does not exist yet, saying so is exactly what we need, because the gaps are what the toolkit fills. Nothing here is scored against you personally. It takes about eight minutes, and you can send it in one sitting.

01

The basics

H1Roughly how many people work here right now, across every branch and site? Count everyone you pay, full and part time.

Two important legal duties switch on around 15 staff, so an exact-enough count decides which rules we build for.

H2Is there a written discipline procedure: verbal warning, written warning, inquiry, sanction?

If anything exists we build on it rather than replace it, so your people see continuity, not upheaval.

If one exists, add it to the upload folder at the end. A photo is fine.

H3When warnings are given, are they written, dated, signed and filed?

Filed warnings are the company's own protection if a dispute ever comes; we check what the current letters look like.

If you have two or three warning letters, add them to the upload folder. Names may be covered.

H4Has the company ever held a formal inquiry (a domestic inquiry) before dismissing someone for misconduct?

If yes, we review how it ran and keep what worked; if no, the toolkit gives you the full model before you ever need it.

If you have one inquiry record, add it to the upload folder. Names may be covered.

02

Unions and outside help

H5Are any of your staff union members, or is there any union presence in the workplace?

Union involvement changes how inquiries and exits are best handled; knowing early keeps every step smooth.

H5aIs there a trade union agreement or any other collective agreement in place that covers your staff?

A collective agreement can set its own steps for warnings and inquiries; if one exists we build the toolkit to fit it, so nothing you do later cuts across it.

If one exists, add it to the upload folder at the end.

H5bHas the company ever brought in an outside person to help settle a dispute with an employee, for example an elder, a mediator or a lawyer?

Knowing what has worked for you before means we suggest a route you are comfortable with.

03

Exits and current practice

H6Do appointment letters state a notice period for both sides?

The notice clause is the starting point of every clean exit; if letters are silent we fix that as part of the kit.

Please add one appointment letter showing the notice and probation clauses to the upload folder. Any employee, name may be covered.

H7In the last two years, roughly how many people left, and how many of those exits were the company's decision?

The recent pattern shows us which exit checklists you will actually use, so the kit is practical rather than theoretical.

H8Has anyone ever been suspended from work while a matter was looked into?

Suspension has legal strings attached; if it has happened or might, we make sure the next one is done properly.

H9When a discipline issue comes up today, what actually happens, in one or two lines?

The honest current picture lets us build a procedure your managers will genuinely follow, not a document that sits in a drawer.
