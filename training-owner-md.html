Where to invest

Prepared for your company · for the owner or managing director

This short form starts the build of your training and development pack. These questions are for the head of the business: where the company is heading, and who it cannot afford to lose. Nothing here is scored, and gaps are exactly what the pack is for.

This is not about finding fault with anyone. It is here to help you spend training time and money where the business is actually heading, and to keep good people by investing visibly in them. The honest answer is always the useful one.

Where the business is heading

M1Which capability worries you most: what can your company not do well today that it will need to do well in the next two years?

Your answer sets the direction of the whole plan, so the training money goes where the business is heading, not where habit points.

M2Name the people the business could not afford to lose. Two to five names, with their roles.

These names get the growth plans and the successor thinking; investing visibly in them is what makes them stay.

M2bFor each of those people: is there someone who could step into their role in time, and what would that person still need to learn to be ready? Write "no one yet" where there is nobody, and please say so plainly, because that gap is exactly what the plan is for.

This builds your succession and continuity plan: a named or to-be-found understudy and the cross-training behind them, so the business is not one resignation away from trouble.

M3Roughly what could the company spend on training in a year without it hurting? A range is fine.

An honest number keeps the plan realistic, so it gets carried out instead of admired and shelved.

M4Does your company hold ISO certification, or face buyer or compliance audits, now or expected?

If yes, the records system we design will be built to pass exactly those audits, which is usually where this pack pays for itself first.

M5If this pack could fix one thing, what should it be?

We weight the work toward your answer.
