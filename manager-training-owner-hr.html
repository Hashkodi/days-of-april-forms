Manager Essentials Programme

What you want changed

Prepared for your company · for the owner and HR

This short form shapes the training around what you want changed. There are no wrong answers, and nothing here is shared with your managers in your name. It takes about ten minutes.

The programme is built from your answers, not a fixed syllabus. Nothing is saved on this device until you send it at the end.

01

Why now, and who

P1What made you decide to look at manager training now? Was there a moment or a problem behind it?

The sessions open on the problems you actually have, not a generic syllabus.

P2Which managers should attend? List names or roles, and how many people each one manages.

Group size and team sizes shape the exercises; the one-to-one rhythm we teach depends on how many people each manager has.

P3Six months after the programme, what should be visibly different in how your managers work?

This becomes the measure of success in your closing report, so we are both judging the programme by your yardstick.

02

How your managers work today

P4Which everyday people-matters do your managers handle themselves today, and which still come to you? Tick all they handle themselves.

Shows how much is on your desk that the programme can move off it.

P5What usually earns someone a promotion to manager here?

Tells us where your managers started from, so the sessions meet them there.

P6Have your managers had any training in managing people before?

If something was tried before, we build on it rather than repeat it.

03

Before we stand in the room

P7May we ask your staff a few anonymous questions about day-to-day management, once now and once about three months after the training? No names are taken, and we report only the overall picture.

Asking the same questions before and after is how you see, in plain numbers, whether the training worked. If you would rather not, the programme still runs, and the anonymous survey is never sent.

P8Is there anything sensitive we should know before standing in front of your managers? For example a recent dispute, a resignation, or a manager who may resist the training.

Nothing you write here is repeated; it simply keeps us from stepping on a landmine in the room.

P9Which day and time of week could your managers realistically be free for 90 minutes without the operation suffering?

Sessions that fight the production schedule get skipped; we fit yours instead.
