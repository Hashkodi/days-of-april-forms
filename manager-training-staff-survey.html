Manager Essentials Programme

How day-to-day management works for you

An anonymous survey for everyone at your company

Days of April is an independent HR consultancy. Your company's managers are taking part in a training programme, and this short survey helps make it useful: it asks how day-to-day management works for you now, and the same questions will be asked again after the programme to see what improved. It is anonymous. We do not collect your name, and nobody at your company sees individual answers, only overall patterns. Please answer as things really are. It takes about seven minutes.

Your answers are anonymous. We do not collect names or email addresses. Where fewer than five people answer about the same manager, those answers are combined with others so no one can be identified. Results reach your company only as overall patterns, never as individual replies.

Which manager

Which manager do you report to? This is the only routing question, and it is here only so each manager can be given their own overall picture.

A manager's results are only ever shown where five or more of their people answered. Below that, your answers are merged with others so you cannot be identified.

01

My work and targets

How much of each statement is true for you?

I know what my manager expects from my work this month.

My manager and I could both point to the same list of what my job is meant to produce.

If my work were falling short, I would hear it from my manager early, not for the first time at a review.

02

Talking one-to-one

My manager talks with me one-to-one on some regular rhythm, not only when something is wrong.

In those conversations I get time for my own topics, not only instructions.

When I raise something with my manager, something usually happens about it.

03

How my work is corrected

When my manager corrects my work, it is about the work, not about me as a person.

Corrections happen privately, not in front of others.

My manager also tells me when I have done something well.

I could admit a mistake to my manager without being humiliated for it.

04

Fairness

My manager hears a person's side before deciding anything against them.

My manager applies the same rules to everyone in the team.

05

How the team runs

I hear news that affects my work from my manager before I hear it as rumour.

Our team meetings end with clear decisions about who does what.

06

In your own words

Both optional. Please avoid anything that would identify you.

What is one thing your manager does that helps you do your job well?

What is one thing about how your team is managed that you would change?
