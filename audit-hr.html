Initial HR Audit

How it really runs

Prepared for your company · for whoever runs HR day to day

This form is about how things actually run, not how they should run. Honest answers make the audit useful: where something does not exist yet, saying so is exactly what we need, because the gaps are what we help you fix. Nothing here is scored against you personally.

This is not here to find fault with you or anyone else. It is here to help HR and the company get streamlined and steer clear of trouble later. It takes about fifteen minutes, and you can send it in one sitting. Where a question does not apply, say so and move on.

Who runs HR

2.1Is there a named person responsible for HR, even as part of their role?

If it is nobody's job, it becomes everybody's problem; we help define it.

2.2Does that person have enough time for HR, or is it always squeezed in?

Tells us whether findings need a person or just a process.

02

Hiring

3.2Does each role have a short written job description?

Written roles make hiring, reviews and probation decisions defensible.

If yes, add one or two examples to the upload folder at the end. A photo is fine.

3.4When you advertise openings, where do they usually go?

Shows whether the right people can find you.

3.5Do existing staff hear about openings before outsiders?

Costs nothing and keeps your best people from job-hunting elsewhere.

3.8Do at least two people assess a candidate before an offer is made?

Protects you from one person's blind spot.

3.10Are references actually checked before a hire is confirmed?

Ten minutes on the phone prevents the expensive wrong hire.

3.11Is every job offer put in writing, with pay and conditions?

A written offer protects the company as much as the employee.

3.12Is the appointment letter or contract signed on or before the first working day?

Signing late weakens the company's position in any later dispute.

3.17When a position opens, is it usually filled within a reasonable time?

Long-open vacancies quietly overload the rest of the team.

3.18If you use agencies or headhunters, are the terms agreed in writing? (Skip if never used.)

Written terms prevent fee disputes after a placement.

03

Contracts and probation

4.1Does every single employee hold a signed appointment letter or contract?

The single most protective document a company can have.

4.5When probation ends, does the employee get a written confirmation letter promptly?

Without it, probation quietly never ends, which helps no one in a dispute.

04

Pay and statutory

5.6Does everyone get a payslip showing pay and all deductions, each pay period?

Payslips answer most pay questions before they become complaints.

5.13Is there a set way things run when an employee announces a pregnancy (leave, cover, return)?

A calm, standard process protects the employee and the company at a sensitive time.

5.20If the Labour Department has ever raised anything, was it fully sorted out? (Skip if never inspected.)

Closed findings stay closed; open ones resurface at the worst moment.

9.1Is there a logic to how similar jobs are paid, written or not?

Consistency here prevents most quiet resentment about pay.

9.2Does someone check pay against the market at least once a year?

You find out you are underpaying when someone resigns; a check is cheaper.

05

Records and files

6.1Does every employee have a personal file, paper or digital?

One place per person means any question about them is answerable in minutes.

6.6Are the files of people who have left kept for a sensible period?

Old files answer reference requests, EPF queries and disputes long after someone leaves.

6.10When a personal file is taken out, does someone sign for it?

Files go missing without this; a simple register means they always come back.

15.2Could you produce headcount, staff turnover and absence figures within a day if asked?

These three numbers are the start of every good people decision.

15.4Does payroll run on software or a controlled, repeatable process?

A controlled process survives the payroll person's holiday.

15.6If one computer was lost tomorrow, would the HR records survive?

One backup decision now saves years of records later.

06

Policies

7.1Is there a written leave policy: how to apply, who approves, what happens to unused leave?

A written policy means leave decisions stop being arguments.

7.2Is there a written code of conduct?

Expected behaviour in writing makes discipline fair and defensible.

7.3Is there an anti-harassment policy with a clear way to complain?

Protects your people, and protects the company the day something happens.

7.4Are there written rules for using company systems and data?

Clear rules make the one serious IT incident manageable instead of chaotic.

7.7Are policies reviewed when the law or the company changes?

Laws move; a policy written once and forgotten can quietly become non-compliant.

07

Performance and development

10.2Do managers and staff sit down about performance at least twice a year?

Regular conversations catch problems while they are still small.

10.9Does each role have clear targets or KPIs that reviews measure against?

With targets, reviews become facts instead of opinions.

11.1Is there a regular way of identifying the skills and training the business needs?

Training chosen against real gaps pays for itself; training chosen at random does not.

11.4Do the people you could not afford to lose have a development plan?

Your key people stay longer when someone is visibly investing in them.

11.7For critical roles, is there someone who could step up if needed, even informally?

The absence answer here is the single biggest business risk we find in SMEs.

08

Staff voice and culture

12.1Is there any regular way of hearing how staff feel: a survey, staff meetings, a suggestion box?

You cannot fix what never reaches you; our audit survey will give you a first reading.

12.5When someone resigns, does anyone ask why and note it down?

Leavers tell you the truth on the way out; it is free intelligence.

12.10Are there regular staff meetings or briefings where the company shares news?

Staff who hear news from management trust management; the grapevine version is always worse.

13.1Is there a written discipline procedure: verbal, written warning, inquiry, sanction?

Written steps protect the company legally and feel fairer to everyone.

If yes, add it to the upload folder at the end, or a photo.

13.6If a staff member has a complaint, is there a set way to raise it besides going to their own manager?

A working channel catches serious problems while they are still private.

13.10When someone leaves, is there a set routine: access removed, property returned, exit noted?

A clean exit protects data, assets and the relationship.

14.1Is there a current organisation chart showing who reports to whom?

We use it to check spans and cover; if none exists, we draw one for you.

If yes, add it to the upload folder at the end, or a photo.

14.7Do new managers get any help learning to manage people?

Most people problems trace to an unsupported first-time manager.
