How is your company's people side, really?

Twelve questions. About five minutes. An instant answer.

Answer honestly; nobody sees this but you and us. At the end you get an instant reading of where your company stands on the things inspections, disputes and growth all turn on, and we send you a short personal note on what your answers suggest.

This is not about finding fault. Most companies grow faster than their paperwork, and every gap this check can surface is fixable. Your answers are confidential and there is no obligation attached to anything here.

01

The paperwork

1Does every employee have a signed contract or appointment letter on file?

The first document any inspection or dispute asks for.

2Do EPF and ETF go out on time every month, for everyone on the payroll?

This is the single item with the most direct cost to the owner when it slips.

3If asked, could you produce any employee's complete file within an hour?

Contract, ID copy, leave record, letters. The hour is the honest test.

4Is leave taken recorded in writing for every employee?

Memory-kept leave is where staff disputes most often begin.

02

The rules of the game

5Are there written company policies staff can actually read, covering at least leave, conduct and discipline?

Unwritten rules are applied unevenly, and unevenness is what gets challenged.

6When someone has to be warned about conduct or performance, is every step put in writing at the time?

What was written at the time is the only version that counts later.

7When someone leaves, do they get their final documents properly: a letter, final dues worked out, a certificate of service?

A clean exit closes the file. An untidy one keeps it open for years.

8When a legal question about staff comes up, is there a reliable, current source the company checks, rather than memory?

The rules change. Answers from memory age quietly.

03

People and performance

9Do new joiners get their letter, EPF registration and a proper introduction in the first week?

The first week decides how the next year goes, on paper and in person.

10Do staff have written targets, or any regular review of how they are doing?

Without a written yardstick, raises and promotions run on impressions.

11If a key person resigned tomorrow, could someone else carry their duties within a month?

The quiet risk in most growing companies: everything works until one person leaves.

12Is pay set on a consistent basis, rather than case by case?

Case-by-case pay feels flexible. It is also how good people quietly conclude it is unfair.
