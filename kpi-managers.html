KPIs and Goal-Setting

Your team's numbers

Prepared for your company · for managers who run a team

Your company is setting up clear targets for each role, and you run a team, so your view of the numbers matters most. This short form asks what you can actually see and influence. Your answers go to Days of April and shape your team's targets; this is your chance to make sure they are set on things your team genuinely controls.

There are no wrong answers here, and nothing is a test of you. It takes about seven minutes, and nothing is saved on this device until you send it at the end.

01

What your team is measured on

G1What does your team produce or deliver that the business depends on?

Targets built on your answer measure the real job, not a head-office guess at it.

G2Which numbers about your team's work can you see today, and how?

We only set targets on numbers you can actually check; this tells us which those are.

G3Which numbers about your team's work can you NOT see but wish you could?

Often a one-line daily record fixes this; you get the visibility, not just the target.

G4What do you already watch, formally or in your head, to know your team is having a good week?

Your informal measures are usually the best early-warning numbers; we put them on paper so they count.

02

What is fair to measure

G5If your team were measured on one number, which would be fair, and which would be unfair?

Targets set on what your team genuinely controls protect you as their manager: when a rating or a decision is questioned, you point to an agreed number, not defend an opinion.

G6Is there anything your team is blamed for that is actually outside your control?

Targets only work when they sit with the people who can move them; this stops yours being set on someone else's problem.
